Process

It's a walnut tree that fell at a piece of
property that has been in my
family for 125
years or so, since 1883. When it fell my
Uncle had it
milled up and dry stacked. This
was about 10 years ago. Since it's
air-dried
not kiln-dried it has way more vibrant colors.

When I brought it in it was testing at 17%
moisture so I had to give
it a couple days till
the moisture dropped to 12% before I could

start working so it wouldn't cup or bow.
